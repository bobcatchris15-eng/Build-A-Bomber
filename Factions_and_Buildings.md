# Factions & Base Building

While the core mechanics of the game (the Designer and the Damage Model) are universal, the player's choice of Faction and how they construct their bases provide the final layer of strategic flavor.

## 🏭 Base Building (C&C Style)
The game leans heavily into classic *Command & Conquer* style base building for its core infrastructure. To prevent the designer from becoming overly tedious, **the only buildings that players custom-design are Defensive Structures.** 

### 1. Pre-Fab Infrastructure
Core base buildings are pre-fabricated and specific to the player's Faction. You simply select them from a build menu and drop them into the world:
- **Refineries:** Resource extraction requires at least one centralized Refinery per base (two for factions that don't autogenerate resources). 
- **Resource Harvesters:** Because of the refinery requirement, all players are pinned into needing some form of mobile Resource Harvester unit to ferry materials from nodes to the Refinery. *(Players CAN design these harvester units in the Armory to make them faster, heavier, or armed).*
- **Factories & Tech Labs:** Pre-fab structures used to actually build your custom blueprints during a skirmish.

### 2. Custom Defenses
Defenses are where the player's creativity shines. You design them in the Armory exactly like you design mobile units.
- **The Foundation (Bunker):** Instead of a vehicle hull, you start with a handful of base bunker types per faction (e.g., Pillbox, Tower, Fortress Wall). 
- **Hardpoints & Tweaking:** You snap weapons onto the bunker's hardpoints and tweak them for specific roles (e.g., scaling up a Howitzer on a tower for extreme range, or slapping four high-ROF Gatlings on a pillbox for anti-swarm).
- *Advantage:* Defenses don't need locomotion (saving massive amounts of weight and energy capacity), allowing players to mount absurdly heavy armor and oversized weapons that a mobile unit could never physically support.

## 🚩 The Factions
Factions in this game are primarily an **aesthetic choice**, determining the visual design language of your hulls, pre-fab buildings, components, and color palettes. However, each faction provides a small, unique **Passive Bonus** that gently encourages specific playstyles without restricting your access to the universal arsenal.

*(Assuming a 3-Resource Economy: e.g., Metal, Crystal, and Energy)*

### 1. Faction A: The Heavy Industrialists 
- **Aesthetic:** Over-the-top industrial pulp. Think exaggerated smokestacks, unnecessarily massive rivets, comically large gears, and bright hazard stripes everywhere.
- **Passive Bonus:** *20% reduction in all Armor Weight.* 
- **Playstyle:** Naturally leans toward creating lumbering, heavily armored dreadnoughts and impenetrable static bases, as the penalty for thick armor is significantly lessened.

### 2. Faction B: The Technocrats 
- **Aesthetic:** Pulp sci-fi retro-futurism. Bubble cockpits, glowing neon fins, chrome surfaces, and overly dramatic laser sound effects.
- **Passive Bonus:** *5% global Speed Boost to all locomotion types, and a 15% increase to Sensor/Radar vision (pushing back the fog of war).*
- **Playstyle:** Leans toward high-tech hit-and-run tactics, fast skirmishers, and superior map awareness to avoid unfavorable engagements.

### 3. Faction C: The Expansionists 
- **Aesthetic:** 1980s space-marine utility. Exaggerated shoulder pads on mechs, chunky standardized geometries, giant exposed power cables, and loud primary colors.
- **Passive Bonus:** *Overbuilt Infrastructure.* All static buildings and foundations are entirely self-powered (they do not drain from the global Energy resource pool). Additionally, their core HQ slowly generates a steady drip of resources even if no nodes are actively captured.
- **Playstyle:** Heavily favors "Turtling" and expansive base building, allowing them to construct massive, energy-hungry defensive batteries without crippling their economy.
